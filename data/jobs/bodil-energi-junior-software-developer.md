## Job description

At Bodil Energi, we're on a mission to turn everyday electric devices into active participants in the energy system. Our software controls a large and growing portfolio of energy units — from heat pumps to electric water heaters — and we're developing the technology that helps balance the power grid and accelerate society's electrification.

We're now looking for a Junior Software Developer (student assistant, 10–15 hours per week) to join our small but highly skilled team. You'll help us develop new features, build integrations for new types of energy devices, and ensure our control platform continues to scale as we onboard more customers and partners.

### What You'll Do

- Develop and maintain features in our energy control platform
- Implement integrations with new energy devices and third-party systems
- Work with our developers to ensure reliable communication between cloud software and field hardware
- Support the deployment and monitoring of devices in production
- Collaborate on improving developer tools, CI/CD setup, and internal workflows

You'll be working with technologies like TypeScript, Node.js, React, Docker, and Google Cloud.

### What We're Looking For

- Currently studying software development, computer science, or a related field
- Hands-on experience writing code — ideally in TypeScript or JavaScript
- Familiar with Git and Docker, and understand basic software workflows
- Enjoy problem solving and curious about how software interacts with real-world hardware
- Like working independently but value feedback and collaboration
- Motivated by building technology that has a positive environmental impact
- Personal projects, open source, or something you're proud of

### What We Offer

- Flexible working hours (10–15 hours/week)
- Office in central Copenhagen, near Nørreport Station
- Real devices and real customers — full development process exposure
- Small, collaborative, mission-driven team
